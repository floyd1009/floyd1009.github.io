A flame containment system for methane flaring at 400 g/s

Design review passed · July 2026 Fabrication released Oberpfaffenhofen test facility Commissioning · Jul–Aug 2026 Thesis submission · Sept 2026

On a rocket propulsion test bench, methane must be flared safely inside an enclosed building. Burned methane is roughly eight times lighter than air, so buoyancy lifts the flame off its axis and drives it into the structure above. Damage begins at 80 g/s. The full test load is 400 g/s: 200 MJ released in a single 10-second shot, with ambient air only and no supplementary oxygen. My thesis is the device that keeps that flame on the axis.

01 · Passive air supply

A bare methane jet entrains only 76% of the 6.86 kg/s of air the combustion demands. The convergent duct closes the gap: a five-equation control-volume model (mass, momentum, energy) solved as a 520-point MATLAB sweep, then extended to compressible flow when the silencer interface pushed exit Mach to 0.39, with the ~7% density error quantified and accepted.

02 · Combustion state

Product gas resolved with Cantera and GRI-Mech 3.0 (53 species) at constant enthalpy and pressure: 2178 K, below the naive complete-combustion estimate because dissociation absorbs enthalpy. That single state feeds everything downstream: exit Mach, wall heat load, tunnel radiation. A 17-cell reactor march confirmed the in-duct bottleneck is mixing, not chemistry, which sized the 19-port injector (Damköhler 0.125 → 0.61).

03 · Thermal survival

In steady state no steel wall survives this gas: the terminal temperature is 2039 K. Survival is transient. The wall's thermal time constant is 59.3 s against a 10 s shot, so a plain 5 mm S235JR wall ends the burn at 568 K, 232 K under the 800 K service limit, before crediting the slot film cooling at all.

04 · Hardware, released

A 1.70 m convergent duct in 5 mm structural steel with 238 Ø8 mm slots: every hole admits air and lays a rocket-style cooling film along the wall. A 19-port showerhead injector shortens fuel-to-air mixing without touching the entrainment momentum, and 20–30 L/s of water injection shields the exhaust tunnel from the 19.3 MW gas load. Full SolidWorks assembly and fabrication drawings, grounded in on-site measurement at Oberpfaffenhofen: laser-cut, rolled, MAG-welded, about €2,500 and three weeks from order to steel.
